27Description
28 k-epsilon turbulence model for incompressible two phase flows.
29
30 Reference:
31 \verbatim
32 Original model:
33 Launder, B. E., & Spalding, D. B. (1972).
34 Lectures in mathematical models of turbulence.
35
36 Launder, B. E., & Spalding, D. B. (1974).
37 The numerical computation of turbulent flows.
38 Computer methods in applied mechanics and engineering,
39 3(2), 269-289.
40
41 Two-phase adaptation
42 Hsu, T.-J., Jenkins, J. T., and Liu, P. L.-F. (2004).
43 On two-phase sediment transport: sheet flow of massive particles.
44 Proceedings of the Royal Society London A, 460, 2223-2250.
45
46 Yu, X., Hsu, T.-J., and Hanes, D. M. (2010).
47 Sediment transport under wave groups: Relative importance between
48 nonlinear waveshape and nonlinear boundary layer streaming.
49 Journal of Geophysical Research, 115, C02013.
50 \endverbatim
51
52 The default model coefficients correspond to the following:
53 \verbatim
54 twophasekEpsilonCoeffs
55 {
56 Cmu 0.09;
57 C1 1.44;
58 C2 1.92;
59 C3ep 1.2;
60 C4ep 1.0;
61 alphak 1.0;
62 alphaEps 1.3;
63 KE2 1.0;
64 KE4 1.0;
65 }
66 \endverbatim
67

69 inside the \dir turbulenceModels/RAS subdirectory
70 inside the \dir turbulenceModels subdirectory
